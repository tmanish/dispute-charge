# Consumer Commissions & the Consumer Protection Act 2019 (India)

For defective goods, deficient services, unfair trade practices, and sellers or service providers who refuse a lawful refund, the Consumer Protection Act 2019 gives Indian consumers a genuinely accessible tribunal system, far more usable than most people assume. Naming it credibly in a demand letter is real pressure; actually filing is realistic for ordinary people.

## What's actionable

- **Defect** in goods, faulty, substandard, not matching what was promised.
- **Deficiency in service**, any fault, imperfection, or inadequacy in the quality or manner of a service that was contracted or required (this covers banks, insurers, builders, telecoms, hospitals, e-commerce platforms, and more).
- **Unfair trade practices**, misleading claims, false advertising, refusing to take back defective goods or refund consideration.
- **Overcharging**, charging above the MRP or the agreed price.

## The features that make it usable (state these; they surprise people)

- **File where YOU live or work.** Territorial jurisdiction includes the complainant's residence/workplace, no traveling to the seller's city. This was a deliberate 2019 change.
- **File online via e-Daakhil** (edaakhil.nic.in), the government's e-filing portal for consumer complaints, available across states/UTs, with e-notices and video-conference hearings. No lawyer required; individuals file themselves routinely.
- **No filing fee for claims up to ₹5 lakh** at the District Commission; fees above that are modest.
- **Deemed admissibility**, if admissibility isn't decided within 21 days of filing, the complaint is deemed admitted.
- **Mediation** is built in, disputes can route to court-annexed mediation for faster settlement.
- **Compensation is on the table**, commissions award refunds plus compensation for loss, harassment, and litigation costs, not just the bare amount.

## Which tier (based on consideration paid, not compensation claimed)

Per the 2021 jurisdiction rules:
- **District Commission**, value of goods/services paid as consideration up to **₹50 lakh** (this is where virtually all everyday disputes go)
- **State Commission**, above ₹50 lakh, up to **₹2 crore**
- **National Commission (NCDRC)**, above **₹2 crore**

The tier is decided by what the person **paid**, not what they're claiming in compensation, a ₹40,000 phone dispute claiming ₹2 lakh compensation is still a District Commission case.

## Limitation period

A complaint should generally be filed **within two years** of the cause of action (the defect, deficiency, or refusal). Delay can be condoned for sufficient cause, but the safe instruction is: don't sit on it.

## The demand letter that precedes filing

Before filing, send the trader/service provider a written demand, it often resolves the matter and strengthens the case:
- The facts: what was bought/contracted, when, for how much, and what went wrong (with evidence).
- The defect/deficiency named plainly, with the term used: "This constitutes a **deficiency in service** / a **defect in goods** under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019."
- The ask + deadline: "Refund ₹[amount] / repair / replace within [15 days]."
- The escalation: "Failing which, I will file a complaint before the District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission through e-Daakhil, seeking the refund along with compensation and costs."

## Helplines and support

- **National Consumer Helpline: 1915** (or the NCH app/portal), registers grievances against companies and many major brands respond through its convergence program; a useful, free pre-litigation step.
- Evidence to assemble before filing: invoice/order confirmation, payment proof, correspondence, photos of defects, the demand letter and any reply.

## When to add this route to a banking dispute

Deficiency of **banking service** is actionable here too, commissions have ordered banks to refund unauthorized transactions, correct credit records, and pay compensation. For a bank dispute, the RBI Ombudsman (read `banking-ombudsman.md`) is usually the faster first escalation, but the consumer commission adds compensation and is the venue when the person wants damages, not just reversal.
