# The RBI Ombudsman (RB-IOS): Escalating Against Banks (India)

When a bank, NBFC, or payment provider won't fix it, a wrong charge, a fraud claim denied, a chargeback mishandled, a failed reversal, an unjustified fee, the escalation that actually moves them is the **Reserve Bank – Integrated Ombudsman Scheme (RB-IOS)**. Since 2021 the previously separate ombudsman schemes (banking, NBFC, digital transactions) are merged into one, "One Nation, One Ombudsman", so the same route covers banks, most NBFCs, and payment-system participants.

## Why it works

- It's **free**.
- It's run by RBI, and complaints against a bank are tracked by its regulator, banks resolve Ombudsman complaints they'd have stonewalled directly.
- The Ombudsman can direct redress including the disputed amount and compensation (within scheme limits), and there's an appeal path.
- Merely naming it credibly in a grievance letter changes how the bank's grievance cell treats the complaint.

## The precondition: do this first

The Ombudsman requires that the person **complained to the bank first, in writing**, and either:
- the bank **rejected** the complaint or gave an unsatisfactory reply, or
- the bank **didn't reply within ~30 days**.

The complaint to the Ombudsman should then generally be made **within one year** of the bank's reply (or of the 30 days lapsing). This is why the written, timestamped complaint to the bank, with a reference number, is the foundation of everything: it starts the clock and satisfies the precondition.

## How to file

- **Online** at RBI's Complaint Management System portal, **cms.rbi.org.in**, the standard route: register, describe the complaint, upload the correspondence with the bank.
- Physical/email filing to RBI's centralized receipt center also exists, but the CMS portal is the practical default.
- What to have ready: the bank complaint reference number(s) and dates, the bank's reply (or proof of no reply), transaction details, and the specific relief sought.

## What to put in the complaint

- The timeline, tight and factual: transaction → complaint to bank (date, reference) → bank's response or silence → why it's wrong.
- The mechanism the bank violated: the RBI zero-liability circular tiers and clocks (10 working days provisional credit, 90-day resolution), the failed-transaction auto-reversal timeline, the mishandled chargeback, the unjustified charge.
- The relief: reversal of ₹[amount], compensation for the delay/deficiency, correction of any adverse credit reporting that resulted.

## Using it as leverage before filing

In the letter to the bank's grievance cell/nodal officer, close with: "If this is not resolved within 30 days, I will escalate to the RBI Ombudsman through the CMS portal." Specific, procedural, and calm, it tells the grievance cell the person knows the route and has the record to use it.

## Beyond the Ombudsman

- The **consumer commission** route (read `consumer-commissions.md`) remains available, deficiency of banking service is actionable there too, and commissions can award compensation.
- For fraud with a criminal dimension, the **cybercrime track (1930 / cybercrime.gov.in)** runs in parallel.
