# Unauthorised / Fraudulent Payments (UK)

For payments the person did not authorise, fraud, a stolen card, a payment they never made, the UK has strong, specific protection that is different from the "something went wrong with a purchase" disputes. Get this framing right, because the rules are favourable and many people don't realise how strong their position is.

## The core protection

Under UK payment rules (the Payment Services Regulations), for an **unauthorised** payment from a card or bank account, the firm must generally **refund it**, and the starting position is that the person is **not liable** for payments they did not authorise. The person should report it **as soon as possible** once they notice.

Key points to state confidently:
- The bank/payment provider should refund an unauthorised transaction, usually **promptly** (often by the end of the next business day after being notified), unless it has reasonable grounds to suspect the customer acted fraudulently or with gross negligence.
- The person's maximum liability for unauthorised card use is **limited** and is **nil** in many cases (for example where the card details were used without the card being present, or after the person reported the card lost/stolen). The provider can't simply pin the loss on the customer.
- **Authorised push payment (APP) fraud**, where the person was *tricked* into sending money to a fraudster, now has its own reimbursement framework in the UK, under which victims can be reimbursed in many cases. If the person was scammed into making a bank transfer (not just had a card skimmed), point them to their bank's APP fraud reimbursement process specifically.

## Report fast and in writing

- **Notify the bank/provider immediately**, by phone to stop the card/account, then **follow up in writing** to create the record.
- The sooner the report, the stronger the position and the lower any possible liability.

## Language to include

- "I am reporting an **unauthorised payment** on my [card / account]. I did not make or authorise this transaction."
- Transaction details: date, amount, merchant/description as shown.
- "Under the Payment Services Regulations, please refund this unauthorised transaction. I notified you as soon as I became aware of it on [date]."
- If APP fraud: "I was deceived into making this payment. Please process this under your authorised push payment fraud reimbursement procedure."
- Request written confirmation and a timeframe.

## Escalation

- If the bank refuses or delays, make a **formal complaint** in writing.
- Escalate to the **Financial Ombudsman Service** after **8 weeks** or on a final response. FOS is free, independent, and frequently finds for consumers in unauthorised-payment and APP-fraud cases.
- Report the fraud itself to **Action Fraud** (the UK's national fraud reporting centre), this is separate from getting the money back but is the right step for a crime.

Because fraud cases can be high-value and stressful, if the bank stonewalls and the amount is significant, tell the person plainly that FOS is the strong next step and that Citizens Advice can help them prepare the complaint.
