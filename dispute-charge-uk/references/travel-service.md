# Travel, Hotel, Rail, Airline, Event, Package Holiday, and Tradesperson Disputes (UK)

These lean on sector-specific rights where they exist, the contract terms, and the card-payment backstop. Find the lever for the specific sector, then back it with chargeback or Section 75.

## Find the lever for the specific sector

- **Flights (UK):** UK air passenger rights (the retained framework derived from EU261) give rights to **refunds, re-routing, and in some cases compensation** for cancelled and significantly delayed flights, plus care (food, accommodation) during long delays. If the airline cancelled or the flight was heavily delayed, name this directly, airlines often push vouchers when a cash refund and/or compensation is owed. The amount of any compensation depends on distance and delay length.
- **Package holidays:** If it's a **package** (e.g. flight + hotel booked together), the **Package Travel Regulations** give strong protection, the organiser is responsible for the whole package, must provide refunds for significant changes or cancellations, and ATOL protection may apply if the organiser fails. This is a powerful lever; identify whether the booking was a package.
- **Rail:** National Rail Conditions of Travel and the operator's passenger charter govern **delay compensation** (Delay Repay schemes) and refunds for cancelled services. Cite the specific scheme.
- **Hotels:** The lever is the **booking terms, the cancellation policy, and what was actually delivered**. Charged a cancellation/no-show fee despite cancelling within policy? Charged for something not provided? Quote the confirmation and policy back.
- **Events/tickets:** Refund rights usually turn on **cancelled vs. postponed** and the seller's policy. Cancelled events generally warrant refunds. Cite the policy and the cancellation.
- **Tradespeople / home services:** The **Consumer Rights Act** requires services to be performed with reasonable care and skill (read `consumer-rights-act.md`). Work not done, done badly, or not to the agreed spec is a breach. The written quote/contract is the evidence; the person may be entitled to a repeat performance or a price reduction.

## The universal backstop: chargeback / Section 75

Almost all of these were paid by card. If the provider won't refund:
- Credit card, £100.01–£30,000 → **Section 75** (read `section-75.md`): the card provider is jointly liable. Strong when an operator or trader has gone bust.
- Debit card, or under £100, or a third-party processor in the chain → **chargeback** (read `chargeback.md`).

Run the provider request first (builds the record, and chargeback expects it), then escalate to the card provider.

## The facts that win these

- **Booking/contract terms in writing**, confirmation emails, the cancellation policy as stated at purchase, the signed quote.
- **Proof of what was delivered vs. promised**, photos, the cancellation/change notice the company sent, delay times.
- **A clean timeline**, booked on X, cancelled/changed on Y, requested refund on Z, was offered only a voucher.

## Language to include

- "I'm requesting a refund of £[amount] for [booking/reference #] dated [date]."
- The specific basis: "[The airline cancelled this flight, so under UK air passenger rights I'm entitled to a refund to my original payment method, not a voucher, and compensation of £[amount] for the cancellation.]" / "[This is a package holiday under the Package Travel Regulations and the organiser cancelled, so I'm entitled to a full refund.]" / "[I cancelled within the stated policy window, so the fee is incorrect.]"
- "Please refund £[amount] within [14 days] and confirm in writing."
- Escalation line: "If unresolved, I'll raise a chargeback / Section 75 claim with my card provider and refer the matter to [the relevant body below]."

## Escalation

- **Flights:** the airline's formal complaint, then the relevant **aviation ADR scheme** or the **Civil Aviation Authority**.
- **Package holidays:** the organiser, ABTA (if a member), and ATOL claims if the organiser failed.
- **Rail:** the operator's Delay Repay process, then the **Rail Ombudsman**.
- **General/hotels/tradespeople:** card chargeback or Section 75, the relevant **ADR/ombudsman scheme**, **Citizens Advice / Trading Standards**, and the **County Court small claims track**.
