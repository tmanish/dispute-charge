# Right of Withdrawal: the 14-day Cooling-Off Right (EU)

This is the EU's signature consumer right and the one most useful for "I changed my mind" situations. For purchases made **at a distance** (online, phone, mail order) or **off-premises** (doorstep, outside the trader's shop), the consumer can **withdraw within 14 calendar days without giving any reason** and receive a full refund. Crucially, **the item does not have to be faulty**, this is a no-questions-asked right to cancel.

## When the 14 days start

- **Goods:** 14 days from the day the consumer (or someone they nominated) **takes physical possession** of the goods. For multiple items in one order delivered separately, it generally runs from delivery of the **last** item.
- **Services:** 14 days from the day the **contract was concluded**.
- If the deadline falls on a non-working day, it extends to the next working day.

## The single most powerful point: the 12-month extension

If the trader **did not properly inform the consumer of the right of withdrawal**, the withdrawal period is **extended by up to 12 months** (so up to 12 months and 14 days). This is a major lever: if a trader buried or omitted the cancellation information, the person may still be able to withdraw long after they thought the window had closed. Always check whether the trader clearly disclosed the right, if not, say so and assert the extended period.

(Also relevant from 19 June 2026: traders offering distance contracts through a website or app must provide a clear, easy-to-find **online withdrawal function**, a "withdraw from contract here" button, available throughout the withdrawal period. If a trader makes withdrawal hard or hides this, that itself is a failing the person can point to.)

## The exemptions: be honest about these

The 14-day right does **not** apply to several categories. Don't assert the right where one of these clearly applies:
- **Transport and dated-service bookings:** plane, train, and concert tickets; **hotel bookings, car rental, and catering for specific dates**.
- **Perishable goods** (food, drink with a short use-by date).
- **Made-to-order or clearly personalised goods** (e.g. a tailored suit, custom engraving).
- **Goods or services whose price depends on fluctuating financial/commodity markets** (e.g. heating fuel).
- **Sealed audio, video, or software** (e.g. DVDs, games) once the consumer has **unsealed** them.
- **Online digital content** (a downloaded/streamed song, film, software) once performance has **begun with the consumer's express agreement** to lose the right.
- **Fully performed services** the consumer expressly asked to start immediately, acknowledging they'd lose the right once complete (e.g. an urgent repair already done).
- **Goods bought from a private individual** rather than a trader.

This list isn't exhaustive, and national rules can vary slightly, but these are the common ones. If the situation is borderline, frame it accordingly and suggest the person confirm with their national consumer authority.

## How to exercise it

- The consumer must make a **clear statement** of the decision to withdraw (email, letter, the trader's online form, or the trader's withdrawal button). Simply sending the goods back is **not** sufficient, there must be a statement.
- The consumer doesn't have to use the trader's model withdrawal form, though they can.
- After withdrawing, the consumer must **return the goods within 14 days**.

## Refunds and return costs

- The trader must **refund all payments received, including the standard delivery cost** (not enhanced/express delivery the consumer chose to upgrade to), normally **within 14 days**.
- The trader **may withhold the refund until** it has received the goods back, or proof that they've been sent.
- The consumer usually **pays the return postage**, *unless* the trader failed to inform them they'd have to, or the trader agreed to bear it. For bulky goods, the trader must at least give an estimate of return costs up front.
- The consumer can handle the goods only as they would in a shop; excessive use reducing value can mean a deduction.

## Language to include

- "I am exercising my **right of withdrawal** under the EU Consumer Rights Directive for the contract below."
- Order details: item/service, order number, order date, delivery date.
- "I am cancelling this contract within the 14-day withdrawal period and request a full refund of €[amount], including the standard delivery cost, within 14 days."
- If the trader didn't disclose the right: "As I was not properly informed of my right of withdrawal at the time of purchase, the withdrawal period is extended accordingly, and this cancellation is within time."
- "I will return the goods within 14 days. Please confirm receipt of this withdrawal notice."

## Escalation

- If the trader refuses a valid withdrawal, and payment was by card, raise a **chargeback** (read `chargeback.md`).
- Complain to the **national consumer authority**, or use a relevant **ADR** body.
- For a trader in **another EU country**, contact the **European Consumer Centre** in the person's country (read `escalation-ecc.md`).
