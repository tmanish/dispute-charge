# Subscription, Free-Trial, and "I Cancelled But Got Billed" Disputes (EU)

A common situation in the EU, and one where the consumer's rights are strong, especially because the right of withdrawal and the rules against unclear or unfair terms both come into play. Run it as a two-track play.

## The framing

Two angles, depending on timing:
- **If the subscription was taken out online/at a distance and the person is still within 14 days**, the **right of withdrawal** may let them cancel the contract outright (subject to the digital-content/started-service exemptions, read `right-of-withdrawal.md`). This is the cleanest route when it's available.
- **For a charge taken after the person cancelled, or an unwanted auto-renewal**, lead with the **facts and the contract**: did they cancel, when, and were they charged anyway? EU consumer law requires clear pre-contract information and prohibits unfair terms and misleading practices, and the direction of EU rules (including the mandatory online withdrawal function from 19 June 2026) is toward making cancellation as easy as sign-up. Reference the backdrop generally rather than citing a precise national provision you're unsure of.

## The facts that win these

- **The cancellation evidence**, confirmation email, screenshot, reference number, and the date. "I cancelled on 12 February (confirmation #X) and was still charged on 3 March" is close to unbeatable.
- **The sign-up vs. cancel asymmetry**, if sign-up was trivial but cancellation was obstructed, that's the grievance.
- **The free-trial conversion**, if the conversion to paid wasn't clearly disclosed or consented to, say so.

## Two-track strategy (the key move)

Run **both** at once:
1. **Tell the trader**, a clear, dated message demanding a refund of the specific charge (and confirmation the subscription is cancelled), citing the cancellation evidence and, where it applies, the right of withdrawal, with a deadline.
2. **Use the card lever in parallel**, because the subscription is charged to a card: raise a **chargeback** for "charged after cancellation" / "withdrawal refund unpaid" (read `chargeback.md`). The chargeback often claws the money back fastest while the trader message builds the record.

**Stop the bleeding first:** cancel through every available channel now, and if recurring card charges continue, ask the **card provider to block further payments to that merchant** so the person isn't charged again while the dispute runs.

## Language to include (trader message)

- "I'm requesting a refund of €[amount] charged on [date]. I cancelled this subscription on [date] (confirmation [#]), so this charge should not have been taken."
- If within 14 days of an online sign-up: "I am also exercising my right of withdrawal under the Consumer Rights Directive to cancel this contract."
- "Please refund €[amount] to my original payment method within 14 days and confirm the subscription is fully cancelled with no further charges."
- Escalation line: "If unresolved, I'll raise a chargeback with my card provider and refer the matter to my national consumer authority / the European Consumer Centre."

## Escalation

- **Card chargeback** (the parallel track), often the fastest refund.
- **National consumer authority** or an **ADR** body for a trader using unfair or obstructive subscription practices.
- **European Consumer Centre** for a trader based in another EU country (read `escalation-ecc.md`).
